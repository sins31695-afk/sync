SS26 — In Three Movements

Every season starts as a single question about how a body moves through cloth. This one asked three.

01 — Outerwear

Movement

Coats and shells drafted from a single question: how much structure can a garment hold before it stops moving with you? Movement answers with raglan lines borrowed from sportswear and a drape usually reserved for eveningwear.

Shop Movement
02 — Tailoring

Structure

Suiting and shirting built on a canvas construction usually kept for formalwear, then relaxed at the shoulder and waist so it reads as tailoring rather than a suit. Wears equally well behind a desk or on a night train.

Shop Structure
03 — Knitwear

Reprise

Knitwear reworked from Sync's first-ever collection, re-knitted in a heavier merino and given a wider rib. A study in returning to an idea rather than replacing it — the same shape, said again with more confidence.

Shop Reprise
