Two rhythms, one line

Sync was founded on the idea that a well-made garment should disappear into how you actually move — not just how you stand still.

Origin

We started in a single room above a shirt mill in 2016, testing how far a pattern could bend before it lost its shape.

Founder Elena Marchetti trained as a pattern cutter before she trained as a designer, and it shows in the way Sync collections come together: construction first, silhouette second, decoration last — if at all.

Ten years on, the studio still works the same way. Every piece begins as a paper pattern draped on a moving body, not a sketch.

Principles

Construction first

Every silhouette is drafted around a working range of motion before a single decorative choice is made.

Fewer, better

Forty-two pieces a season, made in small runs, so each one gets the attention a larger house can't afford to give.

Traceable, always

Every mill and workshop we use is named on the label. If we wouldn't put our name to it, we don't make it.

The Studio

A small team across three cities, working to one standard.
